## 📖 Overview

This repository accompanies **Part 3** of my three-part series on **Unsupervised Learning**, written as part of my **Relearning ML in Public** journey.

The first two articles introduced the philosophy of unsupervised learning and explored how algorithms like **K-Means**, **DBSCAN**, and **PCA** uncover hidden structure in unlabeled data.

This final article takes a step back to look at the broader picture.

Instead of focusing on individual algorithms, it explores how unsupervised learning extends beyond clustering into **representation learning**, **association rule learning**, and the many real-world applications that quietly power modern technology.

More importantly, it ties together the key ideas from the entire series and highlights why unsupervised learning is fundamentally about **discovery rather than prediction**.

By the end of this article, you'll understand:

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Unsupervised learning is about **discovering hidden structure**, not predicting predefined labels.
- Clustering is only one branch of a much larger field.
- Representation learning allows models to capture meaningful patterns without supervision.
- Association Rule Learning uncovers hidden relationships within large datasets.
- Most real-world data is unlabeled, making unsupervised learning one of the most practical paradigms in machine learning.
- The effectiveness of an algorithm depends on its assumptions about the data rather than the algorithm itself.
- Understanding the problem is often more important than memorizing the solution.
