## 📖 Overview

This repository accompanies **Part 1** of my three-part series on **Unsupervised Learning**, written as part of my **Relearning ML in Public** journey.

Instead of immediately diving into algorithms like K-Means or DBSCAN, this article focuses on building an intuition for what unsupervised learning actually is and why it represents a fundamentally different way of thinking about machine learning.

Rather than asking a model to **predict an answer**, unsupervised learning asks a more fundamental question:

> **Is there any hidden structure in this data at all?**

That single shift changes everything.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this article, you'll understand:

- Why unsupervised learning exists
- How it differs from supervised learning
- Prediction vs. Discovery
- Hidden structure in data
- Geometry in Machine Learning
- Similarity and distance metrics
- Why labels are often unavailable in real-world datasets

## 💡 Key Takeaways

- Machine learning isn't always about prediction.
- Unsupervised learning focuses on discovering hidden structure.
- Geometry plays a central role in understanding data.
- Similarity is the foundation of many unsupervised algorithms.
- Different algorithms define "structure" in different ways.
